Rules of Engagement

ICTAK CTF Specific Rules
  • This is a CTF conducted by ICTAK — no unwanted actions should be performed.
  • Do not share flags under any circumstances.
  • Each participant receives specific flags for challenges. Sharing them can get you caught.
  • Only valid flags are accepted on the user submission portal for every challenge.
  • For Web Challenges, before beginning go to port 7777 of every IP and enter your registered email (used during registration) before solving. This ensures correct flag validation.
  • Flags can be of three types: ICTAK{.....}, CTF{.....}, or Flag{.....}.
General Rules
  • Respect all participants. Any form of harassment or abuse will not be tolerated.
  • Do not attempt to attack the CTF platform infrastructure itself.
  • Flags follow the given format unless specified otherwise.
  • Sharing flags, writeups, or solutions during the competition is prohibited.
Technical Rules
  • No brute force attacks unless explicitly allowed by a challenge.
  • Denial-of-Service (DoS/DDoS) attacks against the servers or other teams are forbidden.
  • Automated tools (e.g., SQLmap, Hydra) may only be used if challenges explicitly permit them.
  • Social engineering, phishing, or real-world attacks outside the challenge scope are not allowed.
Fair Play
  • Play honestly and solve challenges with your own skills.
  • Collaboration within your team is allowed, but inter-team collaboration is not.
  • Any attempt to disrupt the event will result in disqualification.
  • Respect the time limits and challenge boundaries.
Conduct
  • Have fun, learn, and help others grow — but don’t spoil challenges.
  • Respect the organizers, volunteers, and infrastructure.
  • Report any unintended vulnerabilities in the platform to the admins immediately.
  • Remember: The goal is learning and ethical hacking, not causing harm.
